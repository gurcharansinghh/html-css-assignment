Sant Jarnail Singh Ji Khalsa Bhindranwala

Sikh religious leader and political revolutionary

Sant Jarnail Singh Ji Khalsa Bhindranwala was the 14th leader of the Damdami Taksal, a sikh religious group based in Punjab, India during the late 1970's and early 1980's. He was known for his charisma and knowledge of the scripture and history of Sikhism. He spread the original values of Sikhism and persuaded people young and old to follow the original rules and tenets of the Sikh religion. He was also notable for his involvement in the 'Dharam Yudh Morcha' and the 'Battle of Amritsar' in June 1984, in which he and other defenders of the faith defended the Sri Akaal Takht Sahib complex against the invading Indian army.

Some facts about Sant ji

  • Sant Jarnail Singh Bhindranwale popularly known as "Sant Bhindrawale" was a prominent Sikh religious leader and activist.
  • He was born on June 2, 1947, in the village of Rode, Moga district, Punjab.
  • He was a student of Damdami Taksal and became its Jathedar (head) in 1977.
  • He was an advocate for the political and religious rights of the Sikhs.
  • He is known for preaching the importance of Sikh identity and self-determination.
  • He led a series of protests against the Indian government in the Punjab region.
  • He was an influential figure in the struggle for Sikh autonomy.
  • He is remembered for his strong sense of justice and his commitment to justice for the Sikh people.
  • He led the Dharam Yudh Morcha, an agitation for more autonomy for Punjab.
  • He was assassinated by Indian security forces during Indian army's invasion on the Golden Temple complex in Amritsar in 1984.
  • His martyrdom is commemorated annually by the Sikh community on June 6.
  • He was regarded as a martyr by the Sikhs and Akal Takht (highest political institution of the Sikhs).
  • He is remembered for his bravery and unwavering commitment to the Sikh faith and Punjab.
